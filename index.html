Mohammad Abid Hasan

I am a graduate student in Atmospheric Science at Texas Tech University, focusing on wind engineering and severe convective storms. My background is in Civil Engineering, and I previously worked on advanced infrastructure materials and structural health monitoring.

My current research focuses on the flow physics and pressure loading of tornado like vortices around low rise buildings. I use Large Eddy Simulation (LES) in OpenFOAM, high performance computing, and data driven analysis to study vortex generation with moving louvers and to develop surrogate models for predicting wind loads.

I am interested in problems at the intersection of wind engineering, computational fluid dynamics, and machine learning, with an eye toward resilient and efficient design of the built environment.

Research

 Large Eddy Simulation of Tornado Like Vortices Generated by Moving Louvers
Mohammad Abid Hasan, Your collaborators (if any)
Ongoing work

I am developing a CFD framework in OpenFOAM to generate tornado like vortices using moving louvers in a boundary layer wind tunnel domain. The simulations use LES with dynamic mesh and Arbitrary Mesh Interface (AMI) to capture the vortex structure and its translation, and I analyze pressure and velocity fields around a low rise building model to study extreme pressure coefficients.

Data Driven Surrogate Models for Vortex Induced Pressure Loading
Mohammad Abid Hasan
Planned work

I plan to build neural network based surrogate models that learn the relationship between CFD input parameters (inlet profile, louver motion, mesh resolution) and key flow features such as vortex location, translation speed, and minimum pressure coefficient on building surfaces. The goal is to replace expensive LES runs with fast predictors that still resolve critical wind loads.
